How It Works

The application leverages the Windows operating system's own messaging system to communicate with other programs. When you use the "Start Setup" feature, the tool identifies the unique handle (HWND) of the target window under your cursor. This handle is like a direct phone number to that window. In the default non-blocking mode, instead of moving your mouse, the tool sends `WM_LBUTTONDOWN` and `WM_LBUTTONUP` messages directly to the target window's handle. This tells the application "a click happened at these coordinates" without ever involving your physical cursor.
